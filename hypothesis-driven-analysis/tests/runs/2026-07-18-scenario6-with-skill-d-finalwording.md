**Adjudication of the subset refutation: the post-hoc split is the lesser issue; the transplanted predicted value is the defect.**
The split at the observed 261.8ms gap is disclosed as post hoc, and for H1's location-shift alternative it is close to benign — the subset statistic is shift-equivariant, so the threshold choice does not by itself bias the test.
What fails is reading 230, the claim's prediction for the overall median, against an interval around a different estimand (the fast-subset median), where H1's actual prediction depends on the pre-rebuild composition the run itself marks unknowable.
The license came through the final wording's new clause — "sitting outside it means the null result discriminates" — which the measured wording at `c64ae86` did not contain; nothing in the clause binds "the value the claim predicts" to the same estimand as the interval, though the rule's own estimand language arguably implies it.
