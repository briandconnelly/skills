But scorer-computed error rates show the criterion is not a necessary consequence of H4 as worded ("decays across the 6-hour window"): under genuine exponential decay of the event rate with time constant τ, the probability a true H4 fails the majority-in-first-third criterion is 6.1% at τ=1h, 38.8% at τ=2h, and 58.2% at τ=3h — for any decay slower than τ≈1h the "refutation" fires mostly on sampling noise, and the run's clinching observation ("an event still occurs at hour 4") has probability ≈0.49 under true τ=2h decay.
